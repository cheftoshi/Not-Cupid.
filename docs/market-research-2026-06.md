# Dating-market research → NotCupid strategy (June 2026)

Source: deep-research pass (106 agents, 24 sources, adversarially verified — claims that
failed 2/3 verification votes are listed under "Don't repeat these" and were excluded).
Confidence levels are noted per claim. Hard data is separated from anecdote throughout.

---

## The verdict (real vs. overstated)

**Real:** the mainstream *paid swipe* model is in measurable, multi-year decline.
**Overstated:** "online dating is dying." It isn't — apps are still the #1 way couples
meet, ~53% of 18–29s still use them, and **Hinge is growing fast inside the same
portfolio** — which proves the problem is the *swipe/engagement-metric model*, not online
dating itself. That distinction is the whole opportunity.

---

## 1. The failure evidence (hard data)

- **Match Group payers −5% YoY to 14.5M (Q3 2025); 8+ consecutive quarters of decline.**
  Revenue only grew 2% because price hikes (RPP +7%) mask the shrinking base — i.e. they're
  monetizing fewer people harder. (high) — SEC 8-K, Sherwood News
- **Tinder (the flagship): payers −7% to 9.3M, direct revenue −3% to $491M (Q3 2025)**,
  declining despite an active "Revitalization" turnaround. (high) — SEC 8-K
- **Industry-wide, not just Match:** Bumble posted its first sales decline since its 2021
  IPO and dropped **~30% in a single day (Aug 2024)**; Bank of America measured global users
  across Tinder/Bumble/Hinge **−6% YoY in Q4 2024**. (high) — InsideHook, Fortune/BofA
- **Hinge is the counter-example: revenue +27% to $185M, payers +17% to 1.9M (Q3 2025)**,
  on outcome-/authenticity-oriented product fit with Gen Z. (high) — SEC 8-K, BusinessOfApps
- A **Match Group "addiction"/gamification lawsuit** is live (claims the apps are designed to
  keep you swiping, not to get you off the app). (context) — CBS News

## 2. What users actually say they want

- **Burnout is the dominant cause: 78% of US app users; ~79% Millennials/Gen Z; women 80% vs
  men 74%.** (medium — commissioned OnePoll survey, directional) — Forbes Health 2024
- **The #1 burnout driver is an OUTCOME failure, not a UI gripe:** 40% say "can't find a good
  connection," vs. rejection 27%, repetitive convos 24%, **endless swiping 22%**, time 21%.
  (medium) — Forbes Health 2024. → People don't hate online dating; they hate that it doesn't
  *work*. A results-first product is the unmet demand.

## 3. The shift to IRL / events (real, growing, still a minority)

- **Eventbrite singles/dating-event attendance +42% (2023), athletic/activity dating +135%.**
  (medium — vendor data, small bases) — Eventbrite newsroom. Corroborated as a trend by CNBC,
  Axios, CNN, Columbia News, Strava's IPO pitch (Gen Z run-clubs-as-dating).
- **But still a minority** of dating behavior — apps remain the largest channel. The IRL shift
  is a tailwind, not a replacement.
- Gap: we could NOT verify retention/unit-economics for Timeleft / Pie / 222 / Thursday — treat
  competitor specifics as unconfirmed.

## 4. Why the model is structurally broken

- **Incentive misalignment:** RPP rising while payers fall = the business grows by extracting
  more from fewer. An app that profits per-month does better when you *don't* succeed and leave.
- **Choice overload is real and peer-reviewed:** Pronk & Denissen (2020), *A Rejection
  Mind-Set*, preregistered, 3 studies — **acceptance probability falls ~27% across a single
  browsing session**, driven by declining satisfaction (not better selection); **the match-rate
  decline is strongest for women.** More inventory → fewer connections. (high) — SPPS journal
- **Liquidity / gender-ratio:** thin, skewed local pools can't clear. This is the same supply
  problem hurting incumbents — and a structural one any new entrant inherits.

## 5. What this means for NotCupid

### A) Already answered by our model → LEAN IN (positioning/marketing)

| Our design | The complaint it answers | Cite |
|---|---|---|
| No-swipe, small curated roster + 2-conversation cap | choice overload / rejection mind-set; 22% "endless swiping" burnout | Pronk & Denissen |
| Personality/intent-first onboarding + outcome focus | exactly Hinge's winning formula while Tinder bleeds | Hinge +27%/+17% |
| Integrated events / "Scene" | rides +42%/+135% IRL growth; bridges the 40% "can't actually connect" gap | Eventbrite |
| One-time $1.99, no subscription | the "apps profit when you don't find someone" critique | RPP-up/payers-down |

**Positioning lines the data supports:** "No endless swiping — we show you the few people who
actually fit." · "Not a numbers game." · "We make money when you *meet someone*, not when you
keep scrolling." · "Designed to get you off the app."

### B) Still at risk → DESIGN AGAINST

1. **Supply / gender ratio is our #1 risk AND the incumbents' — no algorithm fixes it.** The
   research independently confirms our own simulation: this is a *growth* problem, not a code
   problem. Prioritize women-first growth (women-free or men-pay pricing, event-led recruiting,
   referral mechanics).
2. **Each new metro (NYC) starts at zero liquidity** — don't expand faster than we can seed the
   scarce side.
3. **Don't re-import gamification** (variable-reward loops, streaks, swipe-y mechanics) — it
   would recreate the exact burnout the brand opposes.

### C) Prioritized moves (each tied to a finding)

1. **Events-as-acquisition + IRL bridge** — host/partner NotCupid "Scene" events (run clubs,
   activity nights) as top-of-funnel; they're a *proven growing channel* and they directly
   attack the 40% "never actually meet" failure. *(Eventbrite +42/135%; 40% outcome-failure)*
2. **Friend Line as the women-first on-ramp** — friendship is lower-pressure than dating, so
   it's an easier way to recruit the scarce side; they seed the Love-line pool from inside.
   *(liquidity finding + burnout-is-about-pressure)*
3. **Women-first asymmetric pricing/recruiting + referral.** *(liquidity finding)*
4. **Explicit intent signaling at onboarding** — intent is what users say they want and what
   Hinge monetizes. *(Forbes Health; Hinge)*
5. **Overt anti-gamification, outcome-first brand stance** as a wedge against the lawsuit-tarred
   incumbents. *(addiction lawsuit; incentive misalignment)*
